## Problem Statement

Om AI Solutions has a 10-part article series on AI in Supply Chain that is currently published on LinkedIn. There is no way to host these articles on the company's own website (omaisolutions.com), which means the company loses SEO value, brand authority, and a permanent home for the content. LinkedIn posts should link back to the company website as the canonical source, not the other way around.

## Proposed Solution

Add a `/blog` route to the existing Om AI Solutions Next.js site with two page types:

1. **Blog Index Page (`/blog`)** — Grid of article cards in ascending order (Part 1 first), each showing the header PNG thumbnail, part number, and title. Cards link to individual article pages.

2. **Individual Article Pages (`/blog/[slug]`)** — Full article rendering with:
   - Header image (full-width or constrained)
   - Blue horizontal rule separator
   - Article body with proper typography (just the body — no sidebar or table of contents)
   - Teal highlights on "Next up:" and "Question for you:" callout sections
   - Navigation to previous/next articles in the series

3. **Content Pipeline** — PDFs are the source of truth. Each PDF is read, content extracted, and converted to structured markdown/HTML files stored in the repo. This is a one-time conversion per article, not a runtime PDF render.

### URL Slug Format
Articles follow the "AI in Supply Chain - N of 10" naming. Slugs: `/blog/ai-in-supply-chain-1-of-10` through `/blog/ai-in-supply-chain-10-of-10`.

Articles are stored as static content (markdown or structured data files) within the repo, not in a CMS or database.
